# ChatGPT NMT Transfer Synthesis

This file captures operating knowledge from the ChatGPT transfer conversation.

It is agnostic unless marked NailIt specific.

## Agnostic operating lessons

1. A fresh run does not mean no context. It means no unlisted assumption carries over.

2. A run manifest is mandatory.

3. A business idea must be stated as input, not truth.

4. Prior research is DOC CLAIM until verified.

5. Codex must use the current repo first for product state.

6. A harness is not a prompt. It is the operating wrapper around model, tools, context, permissions, loops, limits, and evidence state.

7. Strong NMT output must expose missing evidence, not just conclusions.

8. Missing evidence includes consideration set, near purchase barriers, price threshold, first aha, respondent type gaps, non buyers, and alternatives.

9. GO means validation.

10. The four truth loops are product truth, research truth, field truth, and application truth.

## NailIt specific lessons

1. NailIt is stronger than an idea because documents claim a real build exists.

2. Build claims remain DOC CLAIM until checked in the actual repo.

3. Product defects are narrower than the entire architecture, especially story repetition and boilerplate leakage.

4. Market proof is still missing.

5. First party contribution database is a moat thesis, not an existing moat.

6. New Frontiers strength depends on proof density, not polish.

7. The top validation target is paid concierge pilots from strangers.

8. Strongest NailIt segment hypothesis is candidates with scheduled interviews at named companies.

9. Key NailIt risk is whether candidates will pay AI assisted company specific prep.

10. NailIt must not become a generic AI interviewer, a live interview copilot, or an employer screening tool.
