# Methodology Preservation Contract

This contract protects the NMT workbench from becoming a NailIt checklist, a generic startup validation checklist, or a weakened replacement for the original Next Move Theory Canon and Skills.

## A. Original NMT Core Concepts

The original Next Move Theory core is the source of truth. The workbench must preserve and operationalize these concepts when a serious run is performed:

1. Next Move Theory as an integrative operating system.
2. Advanced Jobs To Be Done / AJTBD.
3. Job as the unit of analysis.
4. Big Job / Core Job / Small Jobs / Micro Jobs where applicable.
5. Job structure.
6. Job Graph.
7. Critical Chain of Jobs.
8. Job-based segmentation.
9. Map of Segments.
10. Segment + Job as one analytical entity.
11. Value creation.
12. Value-creation mechanics.
13. Aha Moment.
14. Barriers.
15. Fears.
16. Habit and existing behavior.
17. Tax Jobs / friction / cost where applicable.
18. Behavior change and switching logic.
19. Consideration set.
20. Alternatives and current solutions.
21. Consideration Activators.
22. Riskiest Assumption Test / RAT / RIT.
23. Assumption stack.
24. Cheapest credible evidence against the deadliest assumption.
25. ABCDX Segmentation.
26. Unit Economics.
27. Theory of Constraints / binding constraint.
28. Subtraction as a meta-operator.
29. Focus / company attention management.
30. Local optimum vs global optimum.
31. Main algorithm / cyclical next-move logic.
32. Cause-and-effect chain to profit.
33. Field validation gates.
34. Product diagnosis for existing products, where local source material supports it.
35. Producer-skill pipeline: nmt-chat / ask-nmt equivalent, nmt-diagnose or manual diagnosis, nmt-market-research, nmt-craft-value-proposition, nmt-product-requirements, nmt-craft-go-to-market, and nmt-analyze-interviews.

## B. Anastasia / Team / Book Enhancement Concepts

The enhancement layer may strengthen the workbench but must never replace the original NMT core. The workbench must preserve:

1. Strong evidence hierarchy.
2. Claim tagging.
3. Prior docs are DOC CLAIM unless verified.
4. No-carryover protocol.
5. Project isolation.
6. Repo/source-first evidence where a repo exists.
7. Validation debt.
8. First pass / self critique / evidence check / missing information / final recommendation loop.
9. Missing-information checklist.
10. Payment evidence vs stated interest.
11. Non-buyer and non-user evidence.
12. Field evidence before product claims.
13. GO means go to validation, not build approval.
14. Cheapest credible real-world test before build.
15. Subtraction before addition.
16. No generic startup checklisting.
17. No feature-first analysis.
18. No demographic/ICP-only segmentation.
19. No broad market-size claim as demand proof.
20. No user opinion treated as payment evidence.
21. Explicit BLOCKED / PROCEED by category.
22. Explicit false-positive risk.
23. Explicit what would kill this idea or product.
24. Explicit what not to build.
25. Distinguish new idea, existing product, live product with weak validation, paid product, scale-stage product, and dying product.
26. Separate controlled concierge validation from self-serve launch.
27. Preserve agnostic reuse for any business.

## E. Anti-Simplification Rules

Codex must not:

1. Replace NMT with generic startup validation.
2. Treat methodology terms as coverage because they are mentioned once.
3. Treat NailIt as the methodology.
4. Treat market size as demand proof.
5. Treat user opinion as payment evidence.
6. Treat stated interest as observed behavior.
7. Reduce segmentation to demographics, personas, ICP labels, company size, role title, or industry without Job Graph and success criteria.
8. Reduce value to features.
9. Start with features before reconstructing Job, Solution, Problem, Job Graph, and alternatives.
10. Treat GO as build approval.
11. Skip RAT because an idea sounds promising.
12. Skip Unit Economics because early evidence is qualitative.
13. Skip Theory of Constraints because the output is not operational.
14. Skip local vs global optimum because a local patch is easier.
15. Hide validation debt in polished prose.
16. Convert DOC CLAIM into fact without verification.

11. Subtraction.

13. Cause-and-effect chain to profit.

## H. Unavailable Or Paywalled Methodology Areas

When local source material is unavailable or paywalled:

1. Mark the item as `UNAVAILABLE_SOURCE`.
2. Do not invent the missing methodology.
3. Do not pretend coverage exists.
4. Preserve a placeholder or gap in `METHODOLOGY_COVERAGE_LEDGER.md`.
5. Use only the closest locally available public-canon principle and label the bridge as `STRONG INFERENCE` or `HYPOTHESIS`.
6. Block outputs that require unavailable proprietary detail unless a lower-fidelity public-canon version is acceptable and clearly labeled.

Known unavailable or partial areas in the local public source:

1. Full 100+ value-creation mechanics catalog.
2. Product-diagnosis algorithm.
3. Per-question algorithms for launch/PMF, scale, saving dying products, positioning, conversion growth, AOV, retention, and acquisition-channel creation.
4. Product and feature idea generation.
5. Goal-setting algorithm for finding real growth points.
6. Demand creation and acquisition-channel delivery detail.
7. Branding on Jobs.
8. Company rollout process.
9. Customer Success and Support built on Jobs.
10. Full unit-economics integration.

## I. NailIt Relationship To The System

NailIt is a project.

NailIt is not the methodology.

NailIt sources must live under:

`docs/nmt/_source_library/projects/nailit/`

Future businesses must get isolated project folders and run folders:

1. `docs/nmt/_source_library/projects/{project_name}/`
2. `docs/nmt/runs/{date}_{project_name}_{run_type}/`

No NailIt source, segment, pricing, market size, channel, moat, validation conclusion, customer assumption, product assumption, or founder evidence may carry into another business run unless that run's `RUN_MANIFEST.md` explicitly imports it as `DOC CLAIM` analogy context.
